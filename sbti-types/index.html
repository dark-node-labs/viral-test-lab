How to compare close SBTI types

Two SBTI results can feel similar when they share the same social energy or emotional rhythm. To compare them, look for the main verb behind the type: controlling, supporting, reacting, planning, disappearing, overthinking, joking, pushing forward, or keeping the peace. The verb usually explains the difference better than the code alone.

For example, BOSS and CTRL both like structure, but BOSS reads more like taking the wheel while CTRL reads more like keeping the whole system under control. GOGO and WOC! both bring visible energy, but GOGO moves the situation forward while WOC! amplifies the reaction. LOVE-R and MUM both care deeply, but LOVE-R turns feeling into romantic intensity while MUM turns care into practical support.
