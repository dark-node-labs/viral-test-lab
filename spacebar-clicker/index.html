How the spacebar clicker works

The timer runs for 10 seconds. Every clean Space key press inside the test area increases the counter. When the timer ends, total presses are divided by 10 to show your presses-per-second score.

Spacebar clicker vs CPS test

A CPS test measures mouse clicks per second, while this page measures keyboard spacebar presses per second. Use both if you want to compare mouse rhythm with keyboard rhythm.

Tips for a better space bar clicker score

Keep the test area focused, sit comfortably, and use repeated attempts instead of judging one run. Holding Space down is ignored because it triggers browser repeat events rather than individual presses.

Spacebar Clicker FAQ

What is a spacebar clicker?

A spacebar clicker counts how many times you press the Space key during a fixed timer, then calculates presses per second.

Does holding Space count?

No. This test ignores repeated key events caused by holding Space, so the score reflects individual key presses.

What is a good spacebar clicker score?

Many casual attempts land around 4 to 6 presses per second. Higher scores depend on keyboard feel, hand position, rhythm, and fatigue.
